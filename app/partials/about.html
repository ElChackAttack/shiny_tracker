Updates - Pokémon Sword and Shield

Preliminary information for Pokémon Sword/Shield has been added to the tracker. It appears, based on the code and what sources such as Serebii, shiny hunting in Generation 8 is largly static based on encounter rate (i.e. catching or fainting). There's no more concept of chains like in previous generations.

Animated and shiny icons are still not available based on my own searches (if you know of a source, let me know). So we'll work with what we have, thanks to the hard work of Pokémon Showdown.

FAQ

  • How do I use this?
    From the "Setup" tab, you can specify the Generation of game you are playing. Generation 1 had no shinies so that is not an option. If you choose Generation 5 or newer, you can specify if you have the shiny charm for completing the national pokédex. Then you can choose the hunting method you are undertaking and head over to the "Track" tab. Optionally, you can type in a Pokémon name for visual motivation of the shiny you are hunting on the tracker page.
  • What is B(n,p) mean?
    It's the Binomial Distribution. It basically means that for each encounter you have already completed, you will have had that particular percentage of a chance to have already received at least one shiny. It's meant to help provide motivation.
  • And the other number?
    It's the Binomial Distribution, but solved for the number of encounters. This gives you the number of encounters until the distribution percentage is at 90%. Basically, in most cases you really should have received a shiny by the time it gets to 90% (well, except 10% of the time, heh), regardless of hunting method. If you haven't, you might consider taking a break.

The odds for each hunting method were taken from various wikis as well as actual experience. There are a few places where we still don't have solid data about the methods, such as chain fishing. It appears to be similar to radar chaining, but it has not been confirmed. Also, Dex Nav is assumed to be a static increase similar to the increase in odds you get in the Friend Safari. As more data is available the odds will be adjusted.

The S.O.S. Battles introduced in Pokemon Sun/Moon appear to use similar odds increases to Dex Nav, so a static 1/512 chance. This will be updated when actual data is released.

All the calculations for the Binomial Distribution are approximate values and provide no guarantee that you will actually receive a shiny. It's all about probabilities. The numbers will also be off by a few here and there based on rounding errors. But more or less it's helpful information.

This site uses a cookie to store your encounter number and hunt settings. That's it. This site doesn't track you nor is any of your information sent back to the server.
